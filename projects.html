Our Projects

Every project we deliver is a snapshot of where vertical farming is right now, and a glimpse of how far it still has to go.



Rootstock Urban Growers, Leeds.

1 / 3
The abandoned warehouse that SUNFARM transformed into a thriving vertical farm. Image from Shutterstock.
2 / 3
Owners Danny and Carey doing a final walkthrough of the completed warehouse conversion. Image from Shutterstock.
3 / 3
A scientist examining the crops in the vertical farm. Image from Shutterstock.
❮ ❯

Rootstock Urban Growers identified a derelict 12,000 sq ft warehouse on a brownfield site in the Holbeck district of Leeds — not a liability, but an opportunity. The building had been sitting vacant for years, and Rootstock came to us with a bold vision: to breathe new life into this neighbourhood eyesore. SUNFARM conducted a thorough structural and environmental assessment of the site, identifying which elements of the existing building could be retained and repurposed, which significantly reduced construction costs and carbon costs compared to a new, purpose build. The retrofit was completed in under eleven months, with SUNFARM overseeing every stage from planning permission through to first harvest.
The growing system spans eight vertical tiers, producing over 2.5 tonnes of basil, rocket, spinach, watercress, and mixed herbs each week, via a closed-loop hydroponic system smartly controlled by SUNFARM's proprietary AI platform.

GreenLeaf Urban Produce, Manchester.

1 / 3
The purpose-built SUNFARM facility on the outskirts of Salford. Image from Shutterstock.
2 / 3
A close-up of the premium leafy greens grown at the GreenLeaf facility. Image from Shutterstock.
3 / 3
An overview of the completed GreenLeaf facility. Image from Shutterstock.
❮ ❯

GreenLeaf Urban Produce approached SUNFARM Consultancy in early 2023 with a clear ambition: to become the North West's leading supplier of premium, locally grown salad leaves, herbs, and microgreens to independent restaurants, hotel chains, and regional grocery retailers. SUNFARM Consultancy worked closely with the GreenLeaf team from the ground up, beginning with a full site feasibility assessment and crop yield modelling before a single brick was laid.

The result was a purpose-built 18,000 sq ft vertical farming facility on the outskirts of Salford, designed entirely around GreenLeaf's operational requirements. SUNFARM specified and oversaw the installation of a 12-tier LED growing system, a closed-loop hydroponic irrigation network, and a climate control suite capable of maintaining optimal growing conditions 365 days a year, entirely independent of outdoor weather. The facility produces over 4 tonnes of fresh produce per week, with a harvest-to-delivery window of under 24 hours.

Meridian Workspace, Bristol.

1 / 3
A Meridian tenant working beside the integrated micro farm installation. Image from Shutterstock.
2 / 3
An overview of the micro farm units installed across Meridian's communal spaces. Image from Shutterstock.
3 / 3
A Meridian tenant harvesting fresh herbs from the office micro farm. Image from Shutterstock.
❮ ❯

Meridian Workspace, a multi-floor co-working and serviced office provider in Bristol's Temple Quarter, wanted to enhance their offering for tenants — they didn't think of vertical farming as an obvious solution until they spoke to SUNFARM Consultancy.
Working within the constraints of an existing commercial building, SUNFARM designed and installed a series of integrated micro farm units across Meridian's communal breakout areas, reception lobby, and kitchen spaces, using available wall space, alcoves, and natural light corridors that had previously gone entirely unused. The entire system runs on SUNFARM's smart climate platform, which autonomously manages watering schedules, nutrient dosing, humidity, and LED lighting cycles around the clock, requiring zero day-to-day involvement from Meridian's facilities team.
Running costs are kept exceptionally low thanks to precision-controlled micro-irrigation, and energy consumption is minimised by AI-optimised lighting that responds to natural light levels in real time. Kitchen waste is collected, composted on a small scale, and fed back into the system.
SUNFARM provides a twice-yearly consultancy visit to recalibrate systems and refresh plant varieties. For Meridian, it has become one of the most talked-about features of the building — a living amenity that costs less to run than most of the office plants it replaced.
