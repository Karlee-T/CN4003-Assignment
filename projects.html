Rootstock Urban Growers, Leeds.

When Rootstock Urban Growers identified a derelict 12,000 sq ft warehouse on a brownfield site in the Holbeck district of Leeds, they saw not a liability, but an opportunity. The building had sat vacant for over six years, and Rootstock came to SUNFARM Consultancy with a bold vision — to breathe new life into the forgotten industrial space and transform it into a thriving, community-rooted vertical farm producing premium leafy greens and culinary herbs for the people and restaurants of West Yorkshire. SUNFARM conducted a thorough structural and environmental assessment of the site, identifying which elements of the existing building could be retained and repurposed — significantly reducing both construction costs and embodied carbon compared to a new-build alternative. The retrofit was completed in under eleven months, with SUNFARM overseeing every stage from planning permission through to first harvest.
The growing system spans eight vertical tiers, producing over 2.5 tonnes of basil, rocket, spinach, watercress, and mixed herbs each week — all underpinned by a genuine closed-loop philosophy. Nutrient-rich compost is collected weekly from twelve local restaurant partners, processed on-site, and fed back into the growing system, eliminating synthetic fertilisers entirely. A rooftop solar array and rainwater harvesting system cover the majority of the farm's energy and irrigation needs, and since opening, Rootstock has diverted over 18 tonnes of organic food waste from landfill annually. The farm has also become a community asset in its own right, hosting weekly workshops and educational tours that have welcomed over 1,400 visitors in its first year, alongside a popular Saturday herb box scheme serving more than 300 local households within a two-mile radius.

GreenLeaf Projects, Manchester

When GreenLeaf Urban Produce approached SUNFARM Consultancy in early 2023, they had a clear ambition — to become the North West's leading supplier of premium, locally grown salad leaves, herbs, and microgreens to independent restaurants, hotel chains, and regional grocery retailers. What they lacked was the technical expertise and infrastructure to make it a reality. SUNFARM Consultancy worked closely with the GreenLeaf team from the ground up, beginning with a full site feasibility assessment and crop yield modelling before a single brick was laid.
The result was a purpose-built 18,000 sq ft vertical farming facility on the outskirts of Salford, designed entirely around GreenLeaf's operational requirements. SUNFARM specified and oversaw the installation of a 12-tier LED growing system, a closed-loop hydroponic irrigation network, and a climate control suite capable of maintaining optimal growing conditions 365 days a year — entirely independent of outdoor weather. The facility produces over 4 tonnes of fresh produce per week, with a harvest-to-delivery window of under 24 hours. GreenLeaf now operates a dedicated refrigerated fleet serving more than 60 customers across Greater Manchester, Liverpool, and the Cheshire plains, with zero reliance on imported or air-freighted produce. Since opening, they have reduced their input costs by 34% compared to their previous contract growing model and achieved full carbon-neutral certification within 18 months of launch — a milestone SUNFARM's sustainability team helped them plan for from day one.

Meridian Workspaces, Bristol

When Meridian Workspace, a multi-floor co-working and serviced office provider in Bristol's Temple Quarter, wanted to enhance their offering for tenants, they didn't think of vertical farming as an obvious solution — until they spoke to SUNFARM Consultancy. Working within the constraints of an existing commercial building, SUNFARM designed and installed a series of integrated micro farm units across Meridian's communal breakout areas, reception lobby, and kitchen spaces, using available wall space, alcoves, and natural light corridors that had previously gone entirely unused. The result is a quietly thriving indoor farm growing a curated selection of practical, low-maintenance crops — including mint, chives, parsley, lemon balm, cherry tomatoes, and a rotating selection of microgreens — that tenants are free to harvest and use daily.
The entire system runs on SUNFARM's smart climate platform, which autonomously manages watering schedules, nutrient dosing, humidity, and LED lighting cycles around the clock, requiring zero day-to-day involvement from Meridian's facilities team. Running costs are kept exceptionally low thanks to precision-controlled micro-irrigation that wastes virtually no water, and energy consumption is minimised by AI-optimised lighting that responds to natural light levels in real time. Organic matter from the building's kitchen waste is collected and composted on a small scale, feeding nutrients back into the system and reducing the need for any external inputs. To ensure everything continues to perform at its best, SUNFARM provides a twice-yearly consultancy visit — inspecting the units, recalibrating the smart systems, refreshing any underperforming plant varieties, and rotating the crop selection to keep the farm feeling current and relevant to the people using it every day. For Meridian, it has become one of the most talked-about features of the building — a living, breathing amenity that costs less to run than most of the office plants it replaced.
